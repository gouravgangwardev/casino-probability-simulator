§1 — Law of Large Numbers

Convergence of Experimental Probability

The Theorem

The Strong Law of Large Numbers (Kolmogorov) states that for i.i.d. random variables X₁, X₂, … with E[X] = μ, the sample mean converges almost surely:

P( limn→∞ X̄ₙ = μ ) = 1

In roulette terms: P( limn→∞ (wins / n) = 18/37 ) = 1

The graph below demonstrates this empirically. Observe how the experimental red win-rate oscillates wildly at first, then converges tightly toward 18/37 ≈ 48.6486…% as n increases.

The gold dashed line = theoretical P(red) = 18/37 = 48.6486%. Each colored path = one simulated sequence. Early divergence gives way to tight convergence.

§2 — House Edge Comparison

Casino Games: House Advantage

The house edge is the casino's long-run percentage advantage per bet — the negative expected value expressed as a fraction of bet size. Every dollar wagered is reduced by this percentage, permanently.

§3 — Bankroll Erosion Calculator

How Long Until You Lose?

Given a house edge h, bet size b, and starting bankroll B₀, the expected number of bets until the bankroll is halved:

E[bets to 50% loss] = B₀ × 0.5 / (h × b)
