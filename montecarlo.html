Monte Carlo Simulation

Simulate 1,000 players each betting red 1,000 times. The full distribution of outcomes reveals the statistical inevitability of house profit.

What is a Monte Carlo Simulation?

Named after the famous Monaco casino, Monte Carlo methods use repeated random sampling to obtain numerical results — empirically deriving probability distributions rather than solving them analytically.

Here we run 1,000 independent player simulations, each executing exactly 1,000 spins on red (P = 18/37, payout 1:1). The resulting distribution of final balances is our empirical estimate of the true outcome distribution.

Theoretical Predictions (default config: $1000 start · $10 bet · 1000 spins)
E[Final Bankroll] = $1,000 + 1,000 × (−1/37 × $10) = $1,000 − $270.27 = $729.73
σ[Final Bankroll] = √1,000 × σ_single ≈ √1,000 × $9.9963 ≈ $316.23
